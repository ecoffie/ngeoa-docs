# NGEOA Strategic Analysis & Build Plan

**Program:** Next Generation Equipment Operators Academy
**Church:** New Life City Center (Goulds, Miami)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-05

---

## Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive gap analysis comparing NGEOA to the GovCon BD Upskilling program structure, identifies funding opportunities, and delivers a 30/60/90 day sprint plan to transform NGEOA from a concept to a fundable, operational training program.

**Key Finding:** NGEOA has websites and a CRM, but lacks the curriculum documentation, employer sales materials, accreditation pathway, and funding applications that make a training program investable.

---

### Critical Missing Pieces for Fundability

1. **Defensible Curriculum** — Documented 8-week structure with learning objectives
2. **Outcome Tracking System** — Job placement rates, employer satisfaction
3. **Grant-Ready Documentation** — Logic model, budget narrative, sustainability plan
4. **OSHA 10 Integration** — Safety certification included in program
5. **Employer Partnership Agreements** — Commitment letters for job placement

**Note:** As a nonprofit ministry, employer pricing/sales materials are lower priority. Focus is on grant funding and employer job placement partnerships (not tuition).

**NCCER Accreditation** — Optional future enhancement. Costs ~$2,000/year + instructor certification. Consider after program is established and if major employers require it.

## Part 3: Credentials & Certifications

### What NGEOA Should Include (Minimum)

| Credential | Cost | Priority | Notes |
|------------|------|----------|-------|
| **OSHA 10 Construction** | ~$25/student | P0 | Required by most employers, easy to deliver |
| **NGEOA Certificate of Completion** | Free | P0 | Internal credential documenting training |
| **First Aid/CPR** | ~$50/student | P2 | Nice to have, not required |

### What is NCCER? (Future Consideration)

**NCCER (National Center for Construction Education and Research)** is the national accreditation body for construction training. Major contractors require it.

**For NGEOA as a nonprofit ministry:**
- **Not required to launch** — Many successful programs operate without it
- **Consider later** — If major employers request it, or for federal grants requiring it
- **Cost:** ~$2,000/year + $1,500/instructor certification

**Recommendation:** Launch with OSHA 10 + NGEOA Certificate. Add NCCER in Year 2 if demand warrants.

### Alternative Credentials (Free/Low-Cost)

| Credential | Source | Cost | Value |
|------------|--------|------|-------|
| OSHA 10 | OSHA Outreach | $25/student | Industry standard safety |
| Equipment-specific | Manufacturer training | Often free | Brand-specific skills |
| Forklift certification | OSHA compliant trainer | $50-100 | Additional employability |
