# Execution Plan: NGEOA Church-Led Workforce Academy

**Companion to:** `PRD-Workforce-Academy.md`  
**Purpose:** Assign work, define benchmarks, quality controls, and skills so teams can **execute** and **measure** progress.

---

## 1. Strategy (one page)

**North star:** Job-ready graduates with safety-first credentials and a **living employer pipeline**, delivered through a **repeatable church-partnered model** that can clone to **five Florida locations** and eventually **activate** many of **70+** congregations as partners.

**Pilot (active):** **Goulds (Miami), Church of the Nazarene** — **Brian** (PM), **Earl Coffey Jr.** (trainer), **Cynthia** (secretary/outreach). **Volunteer-only** until funding; **no equipment** secured yet. **Multi-site and 70+ network expansion paused** until go/no-go gates in `RESEARCH-GATES-AND-GO-NO-GO.md` are met.

**PM execution detail:** See **`BRIAN-CHARTER-WEEKS-1-4.md`** (daily touch target, LOS tracker, weekly board one-pager).

**Execution formula (hold constant):**  
`Students → Training → Jobs → Funding → Scale`

**Disciplines that do not scale without tooling:**

1. **Daily** employer outreach and logging  
2. **Weekly** milestone reviews (enrollment, safety, placement)  
3. **Monthly** financial and grant compliance review  
4. **Quarterly** site health audit (quality + risk)

## 2. Phases & gates

| Phase | When | Exit criteria (benchmarks) |
|-------|------|----------------------------|
| **P0 — Mobilize** | Weeks 0–2 | Charter signed; PM named; RACI published; bank/sponsor path chosen; risk register opened |
| **P1 — 30-day launch sprint** | Days 1–30 | 3–5 employer partners; 10–15 enrolled; training live; interviews scheduled in pipeline; tracking system live |
| **P2 — Cohort delivery** | Weeks 1–12 of first cohort | OSHA-aligned modules delivered; hands-on hours met; mid-cohort employer touchpoints; no unresolved high-severity safety issues |
| **P3 — Placement & proof** | End of cohort + 90 days | Placement rate vs target; wage data captured; employer feedback; case studies for funders |
| **P4 — Site #2 template** | +1 quarter | Playbook updated; trainer shadowing; duplicate CRM/site IDs |
| **P5 — Five locations** | Years 2–3 (indicative) | **On hold** until pilot stop sign — five **host churches** planned when resumed |
| **P6 — Network scale** | Years 3–5 | **On hold** — up to **70+** Florida churches remain a **future** engagement model |

Gates are **fail-closed**: do not expand locations if prior site misses safety, financial, or data minimums two quarters in a row. **First cohort date:** not fixed until equipment + instructor + insurance + pipeline gates pass (soft June is **not** a committed launch).

## 5. Benchmarks & “on track” signals

### 5.1 Weekly scorecard (minimum)

| KPI | Green | Amber | Red |
|-----|-------|-------|-----|
| Employer calls logged (weekly) | ≥90% of plan | 70–89% | <70% |
| New employer commitments (cumulative) | On pace for 3–5 by week 2 | Behind 1 | Behind >1 |
| Enrollment vs target | ≥80% of goal by week 2 | 50–79% | <50% |
| Attendance (students) | ≥90% session | 75–89% | <75% |
| Safety incidents | 0 serious | 1 minor | Any serious |
| Budget variance (program) | ≤5% | 5–10% | >10% |

### 5.2 Quality controls (QC)

| QC | Method | Frequency |
|----|--------|-----------|
| **Instructional quality** | Rubric-based practical eval; external SME spot-check | Mid + end cohort |
| **Safety** | Daily briefing sign-in; PPE audit; near-miss log | Daily / weekly |
| **Data integrity** | Duplicate CRM review; placement verification (offer letter or HR confirm) | Weekly |
| **Partner experience** | Employer survey (short) | End of cohort |
| **Financial** | Grant allowability check; receipt attachment | Monthly |
| **Governance** | Board or advisory minutes for major commitments | Quarterly |

## 6. Skills matrix (train or hire)

| Role | Core skills | “Level up” options |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Program Manager** | Project management, CRM discipline, stakeholder comms, workforce lexicon | PMP or Agile cert; local workforce board navigation |
| **Operations** | Equipment logistics, vendor mgmt, site safety coordination | Equipment rental contracts; DOT/site rules as applicable |
| **Instructor** | Heavy equipment operation, adult learning, OSHA-aligned delivery | NCCER or equivalent; train-the-trainer |
| **Admin** | Enrollment, document control, light bookkeeping | Grant writing support; Salesforce/Airtable |
| **ED / partnerships** | Fundraising narrative, employer C-suite comms | Nonprofit finance 101; public workforce funding |
| **Finance (part-time OK)** | Fund accounting, grant reporting | QuickBooks nonprofit; audit readiness |

**Cross-cutting (all leads):** implicit bias awareness in screening; trauma-informed communication; clear boundaries church/employer/student.

## 7. Agents (human + optional automation)

**Human “agents” (named roles you assign):**

- **Pipeline agent:** owns employer CRM hygiene.  
- **Cohort agent:** owns student success and attendance.  
- **Compliance agent:** owns files, letters, grant deadlines.  
- **Finance agent:** owns budget vs actual.

**Automation agents (later / optional):**

- Email parsers or form links that **update task status** from replies.  
- Scheduled reminders (3/7/14-day follow-ups on stalled deals).  
- Weekly rollup email to ED with RAG dashboard snapshot.
