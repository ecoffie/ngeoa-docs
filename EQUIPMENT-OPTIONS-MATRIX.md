# Equipment Access — Options Matrix (Board + Brian)

**Pilot:** Goulds (Miami), Church of the Nazarene  
**Constraint:** **No equipment access today** — must secure **donation, loan, rental, or partner yard** before cohort hands-on.

**Purpose:** Compare paths for **access**, **cost**, **insurance**, **liability**, and **timeline**. Board selects primary + backup.

---

## Options summary

| Option | Description | Pros | Cons | Insurance / legal notes | Rough cost |
|--------|-------------|------|------|-------------------------|------------|
| **A — Donation** | OEM, dealer, or contractor donates or titles equipment to church | Low ongoing cost; strong story for donors | Maintenance, storage, liability; need mechanic budget | **Title, gift letter,** named insured; storage zoning | $0 acquisition; ongoing maintenance TBD |
| **B — Long-term loan** | Written loan: owner retains title; church maintains/covers insurance | No purchase; relationship-based | Owner risk; exit clauses; maintenance | Contract: **additional insured**, maintenance responsibility, return condition | Often $0 rent; insurance + maintenance |
| **C — Short-term rental** | Monthly rental for training windows | Predictable for pilot dates | Expensive; delivery; still need qualified operator | Rental company **loss damage waiver**; verify training use allowed | Market daily/monthly — research local |
| **D — Partner yard** | Train at employer or school site (MOU) | Real jobsite context; may use their insurance framework | Logistics; scheduling; branding | **MOU** with roles, waivers, COI exchange | Often $0–fee; negotiate |
| **E — Hybrid** | Classroom at church + hands-on only at D | Reduces church liability on machine hours | Two sites; transport | Split coverage by location | Varies |
