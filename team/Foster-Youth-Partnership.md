## Why Foster Youth?

### The Problem
- ~400 youth age out of foster care in Miami-Dade annually
- Only 50% employed by age 24
- 20% experience homelessness after aging out
- Limited access to career training and support networks

### The Opportunity
- Foster youth are a **priority population** for most workforce grants
- Opens access to dedicated funding streams (see grant list below)
- Strong mission alignment with faith-based nonprofit
- Creates measurable community impact for grant reporting

## Student Eligibility Criteria

### Foster Youth Priority Track
- Ages 18-24
- Current or former foster youth (aged out or extended foster care)
- Miami-Dade County resident
- Interested in construction/heavy equipment career
- Able to commit to 8-week program

### Documentation Needed
- Proof of foster care status (DCF letter, court documents, or case manager verification)
- Government-issued ID
- Proof of Miami-Dade residency
