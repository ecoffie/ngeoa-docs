## Why Foster Youth?

### The Problem
- ~400 youth age out of foster care in Miami-Dade annually
- Only 50% employed by age 24
- 20% experience homelessness after aging out
- Limited access to career training and support networks

### The Opportunity
- Foster youth are a **priority population** for most workforce grants
- Opens access to dedicated funding streams (see grant list below)
- Strong mission alignment with faith-based nonprofit
- Creates measurable community impact for grant reporting

## Student Eligibility Criteria

### Foster Youth Priority Track
- Ages 18-24
- Current or former foster youth (aged out or extended foster care)
- Miami-Dade County resident
- Interested in construction/heavy equipment career
- Able to commit to 8-week program

### Documentation Needed
- Proof of foster care status (DCF letter, court documents, or case manager verification)
- Government-issued ID
- Proof of Miami-Dade residency

## Marketing to Foster Youth

### Messaging That Works
- "Free training, real career" (no debt, no tuition)
- "Earn $18-25/hour" (concrete outcome)
- "8 weeks to a new life" (short commitment)
- "We support you" (wraparound services)

### Channels
- Foster Parent Association newsletter/meetings
- DCF Independent Living case managers
- Social media (Instagram, TikTok)
- Community events
- Word of mouth from graduates

## Tracking & Reporting

### Data to Collect
For grant reporting, track foster youth separately:

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| Foster youth enrolled | # with documented foster care status |
| Foster youth completed | # who finished 8-week program |
| Foster youth certified | # who earned OSHA 10 |
| Foster youth employed | # placed in jobs within 90 days |
| Starting wage | Average hourly wage at placement |
| 6-month retention | # still employed at 6 months |

### Reporting Schedule
- Monthly: Enrollment and progress updates to Cynthia/FPA
- Quarterly: Outcomes report for grant funders
- Annually: Impact summary for all partners
