**RE: Letter of Support for Next Generation Equipment Operators Academy (NGEOA)**

Dear [Recipient],

On behalf of the Foster Parent Association of Miami, I am writing to express our strong support for the Next Generation Equipment Operators Academy (NGEOA) and their heavy equipment operator training program.

**The Need**

Each year, hundreds of young adults in Miami-Dade County age out of the foster care system with limited employment prospects. Many lack the job skills, credentials, and support networks needed to achieve economic self-sufficiency. According to national data, only 50% of foster youth are employed by age 24, and many remain in low-wage positions without career advancement opportunities.

**The Solution**

NGEOA addresses this critical need by providing an 8-week, hands-on heavy equipment operator training program that prepares young adults for well-paying careers in construction. The program includes:

- Training on excavators, loaders, backhoes, and skid steers
- OSHA 10 Construction Safety Certification
- Job placement assistance with local employers
- Supportive services for program completion

Heavy equipment operators in South Florida earn $18-25 per hour, providing a pathway to family-sustaining wages and long-term career growth.

**Our Partnership**

The Foster Parent Association of Miami commits to:

- Referring eligible foster youth ages 18-24 to NGEOA
- Promoting the program through our network of foster families
- Connecting NGEOA with Independent Living Programs and other youth-serving organizations
- Providing ongoing support to foster youth enrolled in the program

**Our Endorsement**

We believe NGEOA fills a vital gap in workforce development services for foster youth in our community. Their focus on hands-on training, industry-recognized credentials, and employer partnerships positions graduates for immediate employment and long-term success.

We enthusiastically recommend NGEOA for [grant funding / ETPL approval / partnership consideration] and look forward to a continued collaboration that benefits the young people we serve.
