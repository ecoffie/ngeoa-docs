## What is ETPL?

The **Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL)** is Florida's approved list of training providers that can receive WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) funding. Being on the ETPL allows:

- Students to use **Individual Training Accounts (ITAs)** to pay for training
- Access to **WIOA Adult, Dislocated Worker, and Youth** funding streams
- Referrals from CareerSource centers
- Credibility with employers and grant funders

### 1. Institutional Requirements

| Requirement | NGEOA Status | Notes |

### 2. Program Requirements

| Requirement | NGEOA Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Leads to recognized credential | ✅ Ready | OSHA 10 (DOL card) + NGEOA Certificate |
| Linked to in-demand occupation | ✅ Ready | 47-2073 (Operating Engineers) is in-demand |
| Completion/placement data | ⏳ First cohort | Initial approval may be conditional |
| Program cost documentation | ⏳ Needed | Per-student cost breakdown |

### 3. Performance Requirements

| Metric | State Target | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| Program completion rate | 70%+ | Graduates ÷ Enrollees |
| Credential attainment | 70%+ | OSHA 10 pass rate |
| Employment rate (Q2 post-exit) | 65%+ | Job placement within 6 months |
| Median earnings (Q2 post-exit) | $15+/hour | Heavy equipment operators average $18-25/hr |

## Application Process

### Step 1: Contact CareerSource South Florida

**Primary Contact:**
- **Organization:** CareerSource South Florida
- **Phone:** (305) 594-7615
- **Website:** careersourcesfl.com
- **Service Area:** Miami-Dade and Monroe counties

**Key Questions to Ask:**
1. What licensure/exemptions apply to faith-based nonprofits?
2. Can we apply with conditional approval as a new program?
3. What documentation is needed for initial application?
4. Timeline for approval process?
5. Are there current heavy equipment programs on the ETPL?

### Step 2: Gather Documentation

**Required Documents:**
- [ ] Proof of legal entity (501(c)(3) determination letter)
- [ ] FL business registration or exemption letter
- [ ] Program curriculum and training outline
- [ ] Credential documentation (OSHA 10 partnership letter)
- [ ] Instructor qualifications (industry experience, certifications)
- [ ] Financial statements (church/nonprofit)
- [ ] Liability insurance certificate
- [ ] Training facility documentation
- [ ] Equipment inventory list
- [ ] Student enrollment agreement template

### Step 3: Complete ETPL Application

**Florida uses the Employ Florida system:**
- Portal: employflorida.com
- Create provider account
- Submit program for review
- Respond to any requests for additional information

### Step 4: Initial/Conditional Approval

**For new programs without performance data:**
- Florida allows **conditional approval** for 1 year
- Must submit actual performance data after first cohort
- Full approval requires meeting performance thresholds

## Licensure Considerations

### Commission for Independent Education (CIE)

Florida's CIE licenses private postsecondary schools. However, exemptions exist:

**Potentially Exempt Categories:**
- Religious institutions (if training is part of religious mission)
- Programs under 100 clock hours (NGEOA is 300+ hours, so not exempt)
- Programs that don't grant credit or credentials

**NGEOA Path:**
1. First, verify if New Life City Center's religious status provides exemption
2. If not exempt, may need CIE licensure before ETPL approval
3. Contact CIE: (850) 245-3200 or fldoe.org/cie

### Alternative: Industry Certification Route

If CIE licensure is complex, focus on:
- OSHA 10 is a federally recognized credential
- Industry partnerships provide validation
- Some regional workforce boards accept industry certifications without CIE
