# Foster Youth Workforce Development Grants for NGEOA
## Heavy Equipment Training Program - Miami, FL

**Program Profile:**
- 8-week heavy equipment operator training
- OSHA 10 certification
- Target age: 18-24
- Location: Goulds/Miami area
- Nonprofit status required for most grants

---

## TOP PRIORITY GRANTS FOR FOSTER YOUTH

### 1. YouthBuild Program (U.S. Department of Labor)
**Amount:** $1,000,000 - $2,000,000
**Fit:** PERFECT - Serves ages 16-24, construction training, foster youth priority

**Eligibility:**
- Public or private nonprofit organizations
- Serves youth ages 16-24 who have left high school
- Must explicitly serve foster youth aging out of care
- Must provide construction training
- Optional "Construction Plus" for heavy equipment operation

**Contact:** YB_FOA-ETA-26-38@dol.gov
**Website:** [DOL YouthBuild](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/grants)

---

### 2. Stanley Black & Decker - Grow the Trades Grant
**Amount:** ~$24,000 average (166 orgs received ~$4M)
**Fit:** HIGH - Construction trades, underserved populations

**Eligibility:**
- U.S. nonprofits, trade schools, community colleges
- Priority for underserved populations (foster youth qualify)
- Construction and heavy equipment training

**Website:** [Grow the Trades](https://www.stanleyblackanddecker.com/impact/people/grow-the-trades)

---

### 3. Workforce Pell Grants (Starting July 2026)
**Amount:** $4,310 per student/year
**Fit:** HIGH - 8-week programs qualify, construction trades

**Eligibility:**
- Low-income students (foster youth qualify)
- Programs as short as 8 weeks
- Must lead to recognized credential (OSHA 10 qualifies)

**How:** NGEOA becomes Workforce Pell-eligible institution
**Student Application:** Through FAFSA starting July 2026

---

### 4. The Walter S. Johnson Foundation
**Amount:** $10,000 - $400,000
**Fit:** VERY HIGH - Foster youth workforce programs

**Eligibility:**
- Youth ages 14-26 disconnected from education/employment
- Foster care system involvement
- Job training with wraparound services

**Past Grants:**
- $400K for "Next Stop: Your Career" disconnected youth initiative
- $40K for foster youth wraparound services

**Website:** [Walter S. Johnson Foundation](https://wsjf.org/grants/)

---

### 5. Conrad N. Hilton Foundation - Foster Youth Initiative
**Amount:** $100K+ (typically)
**Fit:** HIGH - Transition-age foster youth focus

**Focus:**
- Transition-age foster youth (18-24)
- Education and career success
- Economic independence

**Website:** [Hilton Foundation](https://www.hiltonfoundation.org/work/our-initiatives/foster-youth/)

## FLORIDA STATE PROGRAMS

### Road to Independence Scholarship
**Amount:** $5,000/year per student
**Eligibility:** FL foster youth ages 18-22, enrolled in vocational program
**Apply:** Florida DCF

### Chafee Education & Training Vouchers (ETV)
**Amount:** $5,000/year per student
**Eligibility:** Foster youth 18-23, vocational training
**Apply:** Florida DCF Office of Continuing Care

### Florida Tuition Exemption
**Amount:** 100% tuition waiver
**Eligibility:** Foster youth 18-28, FL vocational programs
**Apply:** Through institution financial aid
